{% comment %} The Apple UI Hierarchy When you open Apple's "Today" view, tasks are strictly categorized in this top-to-bottom order: 1. ⚠️ The Overdue Bucket (Top of the screen, but distinct) Apple groups all overdue tasks together at the very top, but with a crucial visual modifier: The text turns bright red. The Logic: They force you to look at your mistakes first so you don't forget them, but they use color-coding (red text) to isolate them from your actual "Today" items. Internal Ordering: Overdue tasks are sorted Chronologically (oldest overdue task at the top, moving down to yesterday's tasks). 2. 📅 Today's Pending Tasks (The Main Workspace) Immediately following the overdue section are today's active tasks. The Logic: These are sorted strictly by Due Time. Internal Ordering: Tasks assigned to a specific time (e.g., 9:00 AM, 2:00 PM) are ordered chronologically. Tasks that are set for "All Day" (no specific time) are lumped together either at the very top or very bottom of the Today section. 3. ✅ Today's Completed Tasks (Hidden by Default) Apple treats completed tasks as historical archive data rather than active list items. The Logic: When you check a task off in the "Today" list, it stays there for a few seconds (so you get that dopamine hit), and then it fades away into a hidden view. Accessing them: There is a button at the top of the screen (the three dots ... menu) where a user can select "Show Completed". If turned on, completed tasks appear at the absolute bottom of the list, faded out with a strikethrough. {% endcomment %}
